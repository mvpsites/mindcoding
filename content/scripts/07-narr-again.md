# It Is Safe to Start Again
`narr-again` · narration · love · target 8:00 · bedtime — ~110 wpm, low, unhurried, long pauses; the listener may fall asleep mid-way and that's fine

---

You can put the day down now. [p2] Wherever you are — let the bed hold your
weight. All of it. You don't have to carry yourself for the next few minutes.
[p2] Take one slow breath in. [p2] And let it leave without pushing it. [p4]

Tonight I want to sit with one sentence. Just one. [p1] It is safe to start
again. [p2]

Something ended. [p1] Maybe recently, maybe years ago and it's only now
finishing inside you. A relationship. A version of a plan. A version of
yourself. [p1] And somewhere along the way, you picked up the idea that the
ending was a verdict. Proof that you chose wrong, or loved wrong, or were
wrong. [p2] I want to offer you a different reading, and you don't have to
accept it tonight. Just let it sit on the nightstand. [p1] An ending is not a
verdict. An ending is information. [p2] It says: that shape is complete. It
doesn't say a single word about your worth. It never did. [p2]

Breathe in again, slowly. [p2] And out, even slower. [p4]

Here is what endings actually cost, and let's be honest about it, because
pretending has a price too. [p1] They cost the future you had furnished in your
mind. The imagined mornings. The plans that had that person, that place, that
plan in them. [p1] Grieving that is not weakness, and it is not backsliding.
You are allowed to miss what was, and even what almost was. [p2] But notice —
gently, no force — that the missing lives in a room of the past. And you are
not asked to live in that room. Only to visit, sometimes, and close the door
kindly on the way out. [p2]

It is safe to start again. [p2]

Safe — because the part of you that loved, that tried, that built — that part
wasn't used up. It's not a candle that burned down. It's more like a skill.
[p1] Every real thing you've ever felt taught your heart a wider range. You are
not starting over with less. You are starting again with more — more knowing,
more tenderness for yourself, better questions. [p2]

And "again" — [p1] again is not a mark of failure. Look at how everything alive
does it. The tide doesn't apologize for returning. Morning doesn't feel
embarrassed about the night before it. Your own breath — the one you're taking
right now — is a beginning, and it's your millionth one, and it isn't ashamed.
[p2] Beginning again is not the exception in this world. It is the rhythm of
it. [p2]

So tonight, no action items. No plan. The plan can find you in the daylight.
[p1] Tonight there is only the sentence, and the permission inside it. [p2]

It is safe to start again. [p2] Let it slow to your breathing. [p2] It is
safe... to start... again. [p4]

If sleep is close now, let it take you. You don't need to hear the ending.
[p2] And if you're still here — one last thing, softly. [p1] The threshold
you're sleeping on tonight, between what ended and what hasn't begun — it is
not a ledge. It is a doorstep. [p1] Nothing is required of you on a doorstep
except to rest, [p1] and, when morning comes, [p1] to walk through at your own
pace. [p2]

Sleep now. [p2] What is meant for you can find you rested. [p4]
