# You Are Not Behind
`narr-behind` · narration · confidence · target 6:00 · ~135 wpm, direct and steady

---

You are not behind. [p2]

I know it doesn't feel that way. Somewhere along the line you were handed a
schedule — nobody remembers exactly when — and it told you where you were
supposed to be by now. The career by this age. The house by that one. The
relationship, the body, the savings, the certainty. [p1] And every morning,
without meaning to, you check yourself against it. And every morning it returns
the same verdict: late. [p2]

Here is the question no one asks. [p1] Whose schedule is it? [p2]

Trace it. Really trace it. Part of it came from your parents' generation, built
for an economy that no longer exists. Part came from a feed that shows you the
single most photogenic moment of ten thousand strangers' lives and calls it
Tuesday. Part came from one offhand comment a teacher made when you were
fourteen. [p1] You didn't write this timeline. You never signed it. It was
installed — input by input, repetition by repetition — until it started to feel
less like an opinion and more like the truth. [p2]

But a feeling of being behind requires a race. And a race requires that everyone
started at the same line, on the same day, running the same direction. [p1]
Nobody did. [p1] You are comparing your chapter twelve to someone's chapter
thirty, your winter to someone's harvest, your unedited footage to someone's
trailer. The math was never going to come out in your favor, because it isn't
math. It's a trick of the lens. [p2]

Now, let's be honest with each other, because this isn't about pretending. [p1]
There are probably things you want that you don't have yet. Real things. That
ache is valid. Keep it. [p1] But notice the difference between "I want more" and
"I am late." The first one is fuel. It points somewhere. The second one is just
weight — it points backwards, at an imaginary clock, and it makes you heavier
exactly when you need to move. [p2]

The truth about timing is quieter than the schedule wants you to know. [p1] The
people you admire were, almost without exception, "behind" at some point by the
very measure you're using on yourself. The business that took off at forty. The
love that arrived after the deadline. The craft that needed a decade of
invisible reps before one visible year. From inside those lives, it never felt
early or late. It felt like Tuesday. It felt like showing up again. [p2]

So here is the recode. Say it slowly. [p1] "I am not behind. I am in sequence."
[p2] In sequence means: the years you're calling wasted were loading something.
The detour taught you the thing the straight road couldn't. You are not standing
at mile zero — you are standing on everything you've survived, learned,
unlearned, and built, and none of it expires. [p2]

And in sequence means this, too: the only clock that matters restarts every
morning. Today you get a vote. Not on where you should be by now — that
question is closed, it's fiction, let it go — but on what you reinforce next.
One hour on the thing that's yours. One honest conversation. One rep. [p1]
That's not falling behind or catching up. That's just a life, being built, in
order. [p2]

You are not behind. [p1] You are not behind. [p1] You're right on time for the
next right thing. [p1] Go do it. [p2]
