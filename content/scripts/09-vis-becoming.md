# Meet the Person You Are Becoming
`vis-becoming` · visualization · spirit · target 12:00 · ~100 wpm effective with long pauses; eyes closed, seated or lying down; screen stays dark/quiet per LIBRARY blurb

**Production:** very sparse pad under VO, −20 dB, no melody. Pauses are load-bearing —
do not compress them. `[p8]` sections are where the listener's own imagery does the work.

---

Find a position you can keep for a while. [p2] Let your eyes close whenever
they're ready. [p2] Take a slow breath in through the nose... [p2] and let it
go. [p2] Again — in... [p2] and out, a little longer this time. [p4] With each
breath out, let the day get quieter. The sounds around you can stay; they just
matter less now. [p4]

We're going to take a short walk, you and I. [p2] Not far. Everything you need
is already inside the room we're going to. [p2]

Picture yourself standing at the start of a hallway. [p2] It's calm here. The
light is soft — evening light, warm at the edges. [p1] The floor is solid under
your feet. Notice what it's made of. Wood, stone, carpet — whatever appears
first is right. [p4] At the far end of the hallway there is a door. [p1] You
already know whose door it is. [p2] Behind it is you — the you that the years
ahead are quietly building. Not a stranger. Not a fantasy. The version of you
that your best days are already pointing toward. [p2] And you're expected. [p2]

Begin walking. [p2] With each step, let one thing fall away that you don't need
in that room. The argument you've been rehearsing — set it down. [p2] The
deadline. [p2] The scorekeeping. [p2] Feel yourself getting lighter as the door
gets closer. [p4]

You're at the door now. [p1] Put your hand flat against it. It's warm — the
warmth of a room that's been lived in well. [p2] When you're ready... open it.
[p4]

Step inside. [p2] Let the room assemble itself around you — don't force it.
Notice the light first. [p2] Now the size of it — is it wide and open, or close
and comfortable? [p4] There are things on the walls. Don't decide what they are;
just look, and let them be there. Something made. Something kept. Something
that marks a moment that hasn't happened yet. [p8]

And there — across the room — they are. [p2] You. [p2] A little further down
the road. Take them in slowly. [p1] Notice how they're standing. The way their
shoulders sit — like they're not braced against anything. [p2] Notice their
face. It's your face, but something has been resolved in it. The question you
carry around all day — they're not carrying it. Not because it was never real.
Because it got answered. [p4]

They've noticed you. [p1] And the look on their face is not surprise. It's
recognition. [p1] It's the look you'd give an old friend who made it through
something. [p2] Walk toward each other. [p2] They reach out — a hand on your
shoulder, or an embrace, whatever feels true — and you feel it clearly: this
person is not disappointed in you. [p2] They are built out of you. Every hard
day you've had is somewhere in the strength of that grip. [p4]

They have something to tell you. [p1] One sentence. The sentence you most need
to hear from someone who knows how it turns out. [p2] Be quiet inside, and let
them say it. [p8]

Hold that sentence. [p2] Ask them, if you want, one question. [p2] And listen —
the answer may come as words, or as an image, or just as a feeling of weight
lifting. All of those are answers. [p8]

Before you go, they want to show you one thing in the room. [p1] They walk you
to it. [p2] Look at it closely. This is something your hands made possible —
some object, some photograph, some open window onto a life that started as one
of your small, stubborn decisions. [p2] Let yourself feel the pride of it, in
advance. Your body doesn't know the difference between rehearsal and memory.
That's the whole secret of this room. You're not visiting the future. You're
installing it. [p4]

It's time to head back. [p1] Look at them once more. [p1] They're not going
anywhere. This room is not a one-time appointment — it's a place you now know
the way to. [p2] They walk you to the door. And as you step through, they say
one last thing — the same thing every time, so you never forget it: [p2]
"Everything you do today... I receive." [p4]

The hallway again. Lighter now — morning-colored. [p2] Walk it slowly. [p2]
With each step back, bring one thing with you: the sentence they told you. [p2]
The way their shoulders sat. [p2] The object your hands will make real. [p4]

Now come back to this room — the real one. [p1] Feel the surface under you.
[p1] The temperature of the air. [p1] Wiggle your fingers. [p2] Take one more
deep breath in... [p2] and let it out fully. [p2] And when you're ready — not
before — let your eyes open. [p4]

Afterwards, while it's fresh: describe the room where you met them. [p1] What
was on the walls? [p1] Write it down. You'll want the way back. [p2]
