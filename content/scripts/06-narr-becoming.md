# A Message From the Person You Are Becoming
`narr-becoming` · narration · spirit · target 7:00 · ~130 wpm, warm, first person AS the future self

---

Hello. [p2] It's me. [p1] Or — it's you. From further down the road. [p2]

I asked for a few minutes because I remember this exact stretch you're standing
in. I remember it in my body. The tiredness that sleep doesn't fix. The question
you keep circling at two in the morning — "is any of this going anywhere?" [p1]
I remember deciding, more than once, that the answer was probably no. [p2]

I'm not going to spoil the details of how it unfolds. Partly because you
wouldn't believe some of it. Mostly because the details aren't the point. [p1]
But there are a few things I'm allowed to tell you, and I've waited a long time
to say them. [p2]

First. [p1] Thank you. [p2] I know that sounds strange, because from where
you're standing it feels like you're barely holding things together. But
everything I have — everything I get to be — was paid for in the exact days
you're living right now. The mornings you got up anyway. The pages nobody read.
The reps nobody counted. The time you were kind when you were running on empty.
[p1] You think those days are disappearing into nothing. I'm here to tell you:
nothing was lost. I received all of it. Every single deposit arrived. [p2]

Second. [p1] The thing you're afraid of — the one you don't say out loud — I
know what it is. You're afraid you missed it. That the door closed somewhere
back there and you were looking the other way. [p2] You didn't miss it. [p1]
The door you were staring at wasn't yours, that's all. Yours doesn't look like
a door yet. From your side it looks like a chore, or a small idea you keep
dismissing because it seems too obvious, too close to you to be valuable. [p1]
I won't say which one. But when it opens, you'll laugh at how long it stood
there, unlocked. [p2]

Third — and this is the one I really came to say. [p1] Stop waiting for
permission to become me. [p2] There was no ceremony. No one appointed me. No
letter arrived declaring me qualified. The distance between you and me is not a
credential or a lucky break. It is a few thousand ordinary decisions, most of
them small, all of them available to you today. [p1] I am not a different
person. I am you, plus the choices. That's the whole recipe. It's almost
disappointing. [p1] It's also the best news you'll ever get, because it means
I'm not a lottery ticket. I'm a direction. [p2]

So here's what I need from you this week. Not a transformation. One thing. [p1]
Take the project you've been protecting from failure by never starting it — and
start it badly. Give me one ugly first version. [p1] I'll take it from there.
I always do. That's literally how I was built: you starting badly, over and
over, until badly became decent, and decent became undeniable. [p2]

Before I go. [p1] The people you're worried about disappointing? Most of them,
in the end, just wanted to see you light up. And the ones who didn't — their
opinion aged badly. Trust me on that one. [p2]

It's good back here. [p1] The room where I'm speaking to you from — you'd like
what's on the walls. You put it there. [p2]

Keep going. [p1] I remember this part. [p1] This is the part where it turns.
[p2]
